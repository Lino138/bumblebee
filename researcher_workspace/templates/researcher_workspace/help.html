FAQs

  • General FAQ
    How do I reboot my Researcher Desktop?
    You can reboot your Researcher Desktop in two ways:
    • Soft reboot; You can log into your Researcher Desktop and reboot it as you would a regular laptop/desktop through clicking 'Power Off', 'Restart'
    • Hard reboot; Hard rebooting is the process of shutting down the Researcher Desktop and restarting it after the power has been completely turned off. This clears the Researcher Desktop's RAM and can be used as a way to reset the system when required. You can hard reboot your Researcher Desktop by clicking the "Reboot my Researcher Desktop" button on the website
    My Researcher Desktop has shut down. How can I restart it?
    If your Researcher Desktop shuts down the website will detect this, and if you refresh the website it will replace the "Open Researcher Desktop" button with a "Start my Researcher Desktop" button. Clicking this will start your Researcher Desktop back up again. Once your Researcher Desktop has rebooted successfully you will be able to access it again
    Why and how should I boost my Researcher Desktop?
    Why: When you require more computation power (more cores and RAM) to carry out your research activities you can boost your Researcher Desktop from the standard compute flavour (4 cores and 16GB RAM) to the boosted compute flavour (16 cores and 64GB RAM)
    How: You can boost your Researcher Desktop by clicking on the "Options" drop-down on the website and clicking the "Boost my Researcher Desktop" button
    Why and how should I shelve my Researcher Desktop?
    Why: If you are not going to use your Researcher Desktop actively for a period of time and you would like to preserve the state of your Researcher Desktop, instead of deleting it you can shelve your Researcher Desktop. This saves the state of the Researcher Desktop to disk and frees the resources to be available for other researchers, and helps us keep this service available to everyone. The saved state can then later be un-shelved to load your Researcher Desktop back to the state it was in before shelving
    How: You can shelve your Researcher Desktop by simply clicking the "Shelve my Researcher Desktop" button on the website. You can get your Researcher Desktop back by clicking the "Unshelve my Researcher Desktop" button, which will relaunch the Researcher Desktop with all your previous data
    What are vCPUs with respect to my Researcher Desktop?
    A vCPU represents a portion or share of the underlying, physical CPU that is assigned to a particular Researcher Desktop. Researcher Desktops are launched in Melbourne Research Cloud where the physical cores are hyper-threaded at 1:2 ratio and each logical core is oversubscribed. Hence a 4 vCPU Researcher Desktop is a single physical core machine.

    When should I download an RDP file?
    An RDP file is associated with a specific Windows Researcher Desktop. You should download a new RDP file every time you create a new Windows Researcher Desktop. You may use the same RDP file to access your Windows Researcher Desktop until you create a new Windows Researcher Desktop. But you can download a new file whenever you like
    How do I access the Software Center in my Researcher Desktop?
    The University's Software Center is pre-installed in the Researcher Desktop. You can search Software Center in the taskbar to open the application
    Can I install software applications on my Researcher Desktop?
    Yes, you can install software applications on your Researcher Desktop just like your desktop/laptop
    Can I mount department shares on my Researcher Desktop?
    Yes, you can. Open File Explorer and go to Network, you will see a prompt ("Network discovery is turned offâ€¦"). You'll need to turn on Network discovery to see devices on the network that are sharing files. To turn it on, select the Network discovery is turned off banner, then select Turn on network discovery and file sharing. Mapping a network drive is discussed in the KBA here
    Can I use OneDrive on my Researcher Desktop?
    Yes, OneDrive is the recommended method of transferring files to your Researcher Desktop. Simply open the OneDrive application by searching OneDrive in the taskbar and follow the prompts to sync your OneDrive account

    Can I share access to my Researcher Desktop?
    This is not currently actively supported, but to give others access, you need to run the following commands in the terminal on your Researcher Desktop:
    /usr/local/bin/accessctl -u [username]@[domain]
    /usr/sbin/usermod -a -G sudo `echo [username]`
    sudo -u [username] /bin/true
    Even with this, the second user's experience will not be the same as yours, as a lot of tailoring has been done to refine your experience on Researcher Desktop
    Can I use OneDrive on my Researcher Desktop?
    Yes, OneDrive is the recommended method of transferring files to your Researcher Desktop. It is accessed through a program called ExpandDrive, which is documented in this KBA
    Can I mount department shares on my Researcher Desktop?
    Yes, the university's departmental network shares are automatically mounted on the Researcher Desktop just like your desktop/laptop. The shares you have access to are based on your login. This is located in the 'Shares' folder from your home folder. i.e. ~/Shares
